Session 2 is committed. The design system is up: tokens, layout shell, and a design system preview page at `/`. But the artifacts are the smaller half of this session — this is where the site got its shape.

## The fork from Squarespace

The most important design fork from Squarespace was my release to build around domains of expertise. I had deliberately built previous portfolio websites to fill in the written details of the positions I had held, but over time it became too much reading. I wanted the new site to be communicative and, to some degree, interactive. Allowing visitors to play with how the data is presented is just the first step toward having a site that communicates effectively with multiple audiences.

## What carried over — and what didn't

I deliberately carried over only the content shell: position details, times, locations, responsibilities, and some achievements. These were not details that spoke to my target audiences the way they used to, so I centered my new portfolio website around cases, artifacts, and live design projects. What I most intentionally refused to carry over was the notion that my achievements should somehow be perceivable without evidence.

## The front end

When it came to the front end, everything was driven by explicit directions for the AI to abide by high standards of web accessibility. We very quickly landed on a color scheme and font family, spacing and navigability. Visitors may land comfortably on any page of my portfolio and navigate to content that shows them who I am professionally — sometimes in real time, as with the design choice to showcase live telemetry from my GitHub.

## What landed

- **CSS custom properties** — full palette, type scale (fluid clamp), and spacing scale in `src/styles/global.css`
- **Layout.astro** — semantic `<header>`, `<nav>`, `<main>`, `<footer>` with a sticky header and skip-to-content link
- **Accessibility** — `:focus-visible` ring, `prefers-reduced-motion` reset, WCAG 2.2 AA across all color pairs
