## The fork from Squarespace

The most important design fork from Squarespace was my conviction for evidence over claims. I rebuilt the portfolio to compete against the assumption that my achievements should be taken at my word. I stopped organizing around position histories and rebuilt this site around cases, artifacts, and live telemetry. Secondarily, my design-in-public concept gathered around my domains of expertise. Learning Design is my anchor, but Engineering and Leadership are vital expressions of my work. I wanted the new site to be communicative and, to some degree, interactive, because allowing visitors to play with how the data is presented is just the first step toward having a site that communicates effectively with multiple audiences.

## What carried over — and what didn't

I primarily carried over the content shell: position details, times, locations, responsibilities, and some achievements. But the simple facts did not land with my target audiences the way they used to. People want a story. Something that threads all the parts together. I've been goal-setting, mind-mapping, planning, and building for over a decade, but I had never built a platform like this before. By page: [Builds](/builds) show how I apply my skills to end-to-end projects; Log shows how I am thinking day-to-day over the strategy and details of development; [Practice](/practice) elevates big professional milestones into the spotlight. The most important thing that I left behind was actually the belief that my story could be told without me. The assumption that my previous portfolio website was speaking for me was obliterated by the AI review. What I built in its place is like a little living piece of me.

## The front end

When it came to the front end, everything was driven by explicit directions for the AI to abide by high standards of web accessibility. We very quickly landed on a color scheme and font family, spacing and navigability. Visitors should land comfortably on any page of my portfolio and be able to navigate to content that continues my story — sometimes in real time.

## What landed

- **CSS custom properties** — full palette, type scale (fluid clamp), and spacing scale in `src/styles/global.css`
- **Layout.astro** — semantic `<header>`, `<nav>`, `<main>`, `<footer>` with a sticky header and skip-to-content link
- **Accessibility** — `:focus-visible` ring, `prefers-reduced-motion` reset, WCAG 2.2 AA across all color pairs
