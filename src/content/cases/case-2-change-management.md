situation: |
  In 2019 I joined Learning Design Solutions, an instructional design department inside Pearson Online Learning Services. Within days I'd hit an early goal — moving from a single learning ecosystem to many — and for nearly three years I built courses across a nationally recognized cohort of research and specialized universities, each with its own program offerings. That breadth became a leadership opening when a long-standing partnership reached the end of its contract, declined to renew, and had to be sunset. When the Lead Instructional Designer went on leave, the wind-down fell to me: more than a decade of files and documentation to review, tag, and package per the separation agreement.
constraint: |
  The hard part was political, not procedural. A year earlier the organization had migrated from Google Drive to SharePoint but kept Wrike as its project-management system — a decade-long trail leading back to every file from the partnership's start. I couldn't negotiate details with the partner directly, since I wasn't yet a Lead when the wind-down began. And the work itself was unforgiving: hundreds of files per course, hours of video and interactive content to review by hand.
decision: |
  I mapped requests top-down — from high-level deliverables to low-frequency archived files across all three programs, some dating back fifteen years. The tagging scheme started simple (Package, Archive, Delete), then changed sharply when the partner invoked a clause requiring us to purge every copy from our own archives. Fewer possible end-states made the internal process cleaner but raised the contractual stakes: a missed file was now a breach, not a loose end. Assets were scattered across five functions — project management, learning design, multimedia, transcription, and QA — and tracing them back to original owners regularly dead-ended in staff turnover, missing files, and inaccurate records.
artifact: |
  Timeline: January–September 2021. Programs: MBA (28-course carousel), MS Finance (26-course carousel), MS Computer Science (25-course carousel). Stakeholders coordinated: Program Administrators, Program Director, Project Coordinator, Multimedia Team. Data volume: 10,000+ files, 500+ GB. Data types: Audio, Video, Interactive Activity. Document types: Syllabi, Course Maps, Media Manifests, Course Revision Logs. File categories: Course Files, Multimedia (URLs, Logs, Source Files), Archived Data.
measuredOutcome: |
  Tracked and tagged 15+ years of files and folders across five functional teams. Securely delivered 10,000+ files (500+ GB) to the partner. 100% on-time delivery against the separation-agreement deadline. Zero-gap completeness — we delivered everything back to the partner, leaving virtually no trace of fifteen years of instructional design work.
engineeringTranslation: |
  A large-scale data-migration-and-decommissioning operation against a contractual deadline — built a tagging taxonomy and chain-of-custody process to trace 10,000+ assets across five functions back to original owners, then executed a verifiable zero-gap teardown. Data governance, not archiving.
